The Solar System is a star system consisting of our Sun, eight planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune), their moons, and numerous smaller bodies like asteroids and comets, all held together by the Sun's gravity. The Sun, Earth's nearest star, is at the center, with planets orbiting it in elliptical paths. The inner, rocky planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, while the outer giant planets are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. This vast system formed billions of years ago from a collapsing nebula and remains a part of the much larger Milky Way galaxy.
